WASHINGTON Unable to rest their eyes on a colorful photograph or boldface.

Dumbfounded, unsure of what to do next. Without an illustration, chart, or embedded YouTube video.

"I've never seen anything like it," said Mark Shelton, a high school teacher from St. Paul, MN.

"There are no bullet points, no highlighted parts. I've looked everywhere—there's nothing here but words."

At 3:16 p.m., a deafening sigh was heard across the country as the nation grappled with the daunting cascade of syllables, whose unfamiliar letter-upon-letter structure stretched on for an endless 500 words.
